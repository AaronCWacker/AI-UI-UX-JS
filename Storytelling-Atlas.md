# 🏛️ Storytelling Atlas

## 01. 🪶 American Indian Mythos
- Story Lens: Creation, animals, sky powers, tricksters, sacred land, survival wisdom.
- Tone: Reverent, elemental, oral-tradition style.
- Note: This is the book's historical category title; treat it carefully as a composite fantasy-era lens, not one single living tradition.

### 01.01 Raven 🐦‍⬛ — Raven is the creator-trickster who brings light, change, and cunning into the world, teaching that wisdom often arrives disguised as mischief.
### 01.02 Coyote 🐺 — Coyote is the wandering trickster whose appetites and schemes create trouble, teaching that foolishness can accidentally become world-making wisdom.
### 01.03 Hastseltsi 🗣️ — Hastseltsi is a sacred talking-god figure tied to ritual, dawn, and guidance, teaching that speech itself can become a bridge between humans and holy power.
### 01.04 Hastsezini 🔥 — Hastsezini is a black-god or fire-associated sacred figure, teaching that darkness and flame can both destroy and purify.
### 01.05 Heng 👁️ — Heng is framed as a spirit power of mystery and sacred force, teaching that the unseen world must be approached with respect rather than conquest.
### 01.06 Hotoru ⚠️ — Hotoru is a mythic spirit-force associated with dangerous supernatural power, teaching that survival depends on humility before powers older than humanity.
### 01.07 Shakak 🌑 — Shakak is a mythic power of shadow, night, or fearsome presence, teaching that terror can mark the border of sacred knowledge.
### 01.08 Snake-Man 🐍 — Snake-Man is a serpent-shaped mythic being whose story warns that transformation, poison, and hidden wisdom often coil together.
### 01.09 Tobadzistsini 🛡️ — Tobadzistsini is a monster-slaying heroic twin figure who fights chaos, teaching that courage is sacred when it protects the people.
### 01.10 Hiawatha 🕊️ — Hiawatha is the culture hero and peace-bringer whose story centers counsel, unity, and the moral power of ending cycles of vengeance.
### 01.11 Qagwaaz 🌀 — Qagwaaz is a mythic spirit or supernatural being whose presence teaches caution at the edge of the known world.
### 01.12 Stoneribs 🪨 — Stoneribs is a giant or powerful mythic figure whose body-like-stone presence teaches endurance, danger, and the cost of raw strength.
### 01.13 Yanauluha 🎭 — Yanauluha is a sacred mythic being tied to ritual power, teaching that names, ceremonies, and roles carry living force.
### 01.14 Thunder Bird ⛈️ — Thunder Bird is the storm-winged sky power whose thunder and lightning teach that nature's violence can also guard cosmic order.

## 02. 🗡️ Arthurian Heroes
- Story Lens: Quests, doomed kingship, honor, betrayal, holy relics, knightly tragedy.
- Tone: Chivalric, tragic, shining-but-haunted.

### 02.01 King Arthur 👑 — Arthur is the once and future king who builds Camelot and loses it through betrayal and fate, teaching that noble order must be guarded from the fractures inside the heart.
### 02.02 Sir Galahad 🕊️ — Galahad is the pure knight who attains the Holy Grail, teaching that spiritual victory belongs not to strength alone but to innocence, discipline, and grace.
### 02.03 Sir Gareth of Orkney 🛡️ — Gareth is the humble knight who proves himself through service before glory, teaching that true nobility is revealed by patience under disguise.
### 02.04 Sir Garlon 👻 — Garlon is the invisible knight whose cruelty and ambushes warn that hidden power without honor becomes cowardice.
### 02.05 Sir Gawaine ⚔️ — Gawaine is Arthur's fierce nephew whose loyalty and temper make him both champion and danger, teaching that honor without mercy can become ruin.
### 02.06 Sir Launcelot du Lake 💔 — Launcelot is the greatest knight whose love for Guinevere breaks Camelot, teaching that excellence cannot save a soul divided against itself.
### 02.07 Merlin 🧙 — Merlin is the wizard-prophet who guides Arthur yet cannot escape his own enchantment, teaching that foresight does not always grant freedom.
### 02.08 Morgan Le Fay 🔮 — Morgan is the sorceress of blood, rivalry, and enchantment, teaching that wounded kinship can become magic sharpened into revenge.
### 02.09 Sir Palomides 🐾 — Palomides is the Saracen knight who pursues the Questing Beast and impossible love, teaching that obsession can become both quest and prison.
### 02.10 King Pellinore 🦌 — Pellinore is the hunter-king bound to the Questing Beast, teaching that inherited pursuits can define a life before the heart has chosen them.
### 02.11 Sir Tristram 🎻 — Tristram is the tragic lover and knight whose bond with Isolde defies law and loyalty, teaching that love can be sacred and catastrophic at once.

## 03. 🦁 Babylonian Mythos
- Story Lens: City gods, storm kings, monsters, cosmic law, empire, divine violence.
- Tone: Bronze, thunderous, ancient-city epic.

### 03.01 Anu 🌌 — Anu is the high sky father and authority of heaven, teaching that cosmic rule begins in distance, order, and awe.
### 03.02 Anshar 🕰️ — Anshar is a primeval ancestor of the gods, teaching that every empire of heaven rises from older, deeper powers.
### 03.03 Dahak 🐉 — Dahak is the serpent or dragon tyrant of destructive evil, teaching that monstrous appetite becomes a prison for the world.
### 03.04 Druaga 😈 — Druaga is a lord of devilish corruption and underworld malice, teaching that false divinity imitates power while serving decay.
### 03.05 Gilgamesh 🏛️ — Gilgamesh is the mighty king who loses Enkidu and seeks immortality, teaching that grief makes even the strongest hero confront mortality.
### 03.06 Girru 🔥 — Girru is the fire god whose flame purifies, judges, and consumes, teaching that sacred fire is both tool and trial.
### 03.07 Ishtar 💘 — Ishtar is the goddess of love, war, and sovereignty, teaching that desire and battle are twin forces that can crown or destroy kings.
### 03.08 Marduk ⚡ — Marduk is the champion who defeats Tiamat and orders creation, teaching that civilization is born when chaos is faced and shaped.
### 03.09 Nergal 💀 — Nergal is the plague, war, and underworld power, teaching that death is not an accident outside the city but a throne beneath it.
### 03.10 Ramman ⛈️ — Ramman is the storm and thunder god, teaching that rain, lightning, and kingship all descend from the same terrible sky.

## 04. 🍃 Celtic Mythos
- Story Lens: Sovereignty, fae realms, prophecy, warrior-poets, sacred groves.
- Tone: Mist, blood-oaths, green magic, tragic beauty.

### 04.01 Arawn 🏰 — Arawn is the lord of the otherworld whose exchange with Pwyll teaches that honor is proven when no witness can reward it.
### 04.02 Dagda 🍲 — Dagda is the good god of abundance, strength, and magic, teaching that generosity and power belong together in the true kingly heart.
### 04.03 Cu Chulainn 🐕 — Cu Chulainn is the warp-spasming hero of Ulster who dies standing against armies, teaching that glory can burn a life too brightly.
### 04.04 Dunatis ⛰️ — Dunatis is a mountain or fortress power, teaching that protection is sacred when it rises from the land itself.
### 04.05 Goibhnie ⚒️ — Goibhnie is the divine smith and feast-giver, teaching that craft, hospitality, and immortality can be hammered from the same fire.
### 04.06 Lugh ☀️ — Lugh is the many-skilled shining hero who defeats Balor, teaching that mastery is not one gift but the harmony of many talents.
### 04.07 Manannan Mac Lir 🌊 — Manannan Mac Lir is the sea-lord and keeper of misty crossings, teaching that boundaries between worlds are tides, not walls.
### 04.08 Math Mathonwy 🪄 — Math is the magician-king whose story turns on taboo, transformation, and consequence, teaching that power without restraint bends lives into punishment.
### 04.09 Morrigan 🐦‍⬛ — Morrigan is the battle-crow goddess of fate and sovereignty, teaching that war reveals the truth kings try to hide.
### 04.10 Nuada 🦾 — Nuada is the silver-handed king who loses and regains sovereignty, teaching that wounded leadership must be remade before it can rule.
### 04.11 Oghma ✍️ — Oghma is the champion of eloquence and letters, teaching that language can bind, cut, and illuminate like a weapon.
### 04.12 Silvanus 🌲 — Silvanus is the wild woodland power, teaching that the forest is not empty space but a living kingdom with laws of its own.

## 05. 🐆 Central American Mythos
- Story Lens: Sun cycles, sacrifice, jaguar night, calendar fate, underworld trials.
- Tone: Solar, intense, ceremonial, cosmic.

### 05.01 Quetzalcoatl 🐍 — Quetzalcoatl is the feathered serpent of wind, wisdom, and culture, teaching that civilization rises when breath, learning, and sacrifice align.
### 05.02 Camaxtli 🏹 — Camaxtli is the hunting and war power of fire and survival, teaching that the bow feeds the people but also binds them to blood.
### 05.03 Chalchiuhtlicue 💧 — Chalchiuhtlicue is the jade-skirted water goddess, teaching that rivers sustain life but floods remember the power to erase it.
### 05.04 Camazotz 🦇 — Camazotz is the death-bat of the underworld, teaching that night has wings and fear can sever pride from the body.
### 05.05 Huehueteotl 🔥 — Huehueteotl is the old fire god, teaching that hearth flame is ancient memory carried into every new generation.
### 05.06 Huitzilopochtli 🐦 — Huitzilopochtli is the hummingbird war-sun who demands struggle, teaching that a people can be driven by divine victory and terrible cost.
### 05.07 Hunapu ⚽ — Hunapu is one of the Hero Twins who defeats underworld lords through wit and sacrifice, teaching that death can be outplayed by courage and cleverness.
### 05.08 Xbalanque 🌑 — Xbalanque is Hunapu's twin and partner in the underworld trials, teaching that brotherhood turns impossible ordeals into transformation.
### 05.09 Itzamna 📜 — Itzamna is a creator and wisdom power of sky, writing, and sacred order, teaching that knowledge is a divine architecture.
### 05.10 Mictlantecuhtli 💀 — Mictlantecuhtli is the lord of the dead, teaching that every journey downward has rules, gates, and a ruler waiting in silence.
### 05.11 Tezcatlipoca 🪞 — Tezcatlipoca is the smoking mirror of night, sorcery, fate, and rivalry, teaching that every soul must face its own reflected shadow.
### 05.12 Tlaloc 🌧️ — Tlaloc is the rain and storm god, teaching that fertility and terror fall from the same cloud.
### 05.13 Tlazolteotl 🧼 — Tlazolteotl is the goddess of filth, confession, and purification, teaching that corruption becomes sacred when confessed and transformed.
### 05.14 Xochipilli 🌸 — Xochipilli is the flower prince of song, beauty, games, and pleasure, teaching that joy is not trivial when it sanctifies life.

- Tone: Elegant, philosophical, courtly-mystic.

### 06.01 Shang-Ti 👑 — Shang-Ti is the supreme heavenly ruler, teaching that cosmic authority watches over kings, ancestors, and moral order.

### 06.06 Fei Lien 💨 — Fei Lien is a wind spirit or wind lord, teaching that invisible forces can move armies, seasons, and fate.
### 06.07 Feng Po 🎒 — Feng Po is the old wind master with the wind-bag, teaching that weather itself can be a servant of divine balance.
### 06.08 Huan-Ti 🟡 — Huan-Ti is the Yellow Emperor culture hero, teaching that civilization is made from medicine, rule, invention, and heavenly mandate.
### 06.09 Kuan Yin 💗 — Kuan Yin is the merciful bodhisattva of compassion, teaching that the highest power hears suffering and answers with mercy.
### 06.10 Lei Kung ⚡ — Lei Kung is the thunder duke who punishes hidden wrongdoing, teaching that heaven's justice may arrive as lightning.
### 06.11 Lu Yueh 🦠 — Lu Yueh is a plague-bringing divine figure, teaching that sickness in myth can become judgment, warning, and cosmic imbalance.
### 06.12 Ma Yuan 🥋 — Ma Yuan is a martial or protective spirit figure, teaching that warlike power must serve order rather than vanity.

### 06.14 Shan Hai Ching 🗺️ — Shan Hai Ching is the mountain-and-sea catalog of wonders personified here as mythic strangeness, teaching that the world is larger than the map.
### 06.15 Tou Mu 🌟 — Tou Mu is the starry mother and dipper goddess, teaching that fate is written in the heavens yet approached through devotion.
### 06.16 Wen Chung 🥁 — Wen Chung is a stern celestial general and minister, teaching that loyalty to order can become thunderous judgment.
### 06.17 Yen-Wang-Yeh ⚖️ — Yen-Wang-Yeh is the judge-king of the dead, teaching that every life eventually becomes a case before cosmic law.

## 07. 🐙 Cthulhu Mythos
- Story Lens: Forbidden knowledge, madness, cosmic horror, sleeping gods, fragile humanity.
- Tone: Dread, dreamlike, unknowable, oceanic.

### 07.01 Azathoth 🌀 — Azathoth is the blind idiot chaos at the center of reality, teaching that the universe may rest on mindless power rather than moral order.
### 07.02 Cthuga 🔥 — Cthuga is the living fire of cosmic destruction, teaching that even stars may burn with alien hunger.
### 07.03 Cthulhu 🐙 — Cthulhu is the dreaming priest-god beneath the sea, teaching that ancient powers can sleep without becoming less dangerous.
### 07.04 Hastur the Unspeakable 🟨 — Hastur is the unspeakable horror linked to forbidden names and yellow signs, teaching that some knowledge wounds the knower.
### 07.05 Ithaqua ❄️ — Ithaqua is the wind-walking horror of frozen wastes, teaching that isolation can become a doorway for the inhuman.
### 07.06 Nyarlathotep 🎭 — Nyarlathotep is the crawling chaos who walks among mortals, teaching that the most dangerous cosmic force may smile and speak plainly.
### 07.07 Shub-Niggurath 🐐 — Shub-Niggurath is the black goat of monstrous fertility, teaching that creation itself can become alien, excessive, and terrifying.
### 07.08 Yog-Sothoth 🚪 — Yog-Sothoth is the gate and the key beyond time and space, teaching that all doors open somewhere, but not safely.

## 08. 🐈 Egyptian Mythos
- Story Lens: Death and rebirth, judgment, solar kingship, family murder, sacred animals.
- Tone: Golden, ritual, eternal, funerary.

### 08.01 Ra ☀️ — Ra is the solar king who sails through day and underworld night, teaching that light must be renewed by a daily battle against chaos.
### 08.02 Anubis 🐕 — Anubis is the jackal guardian of embalming and judgment, teaching that death requires guidance, dignity, and measured truth.
### 08.03 Apep 🐍 — Apep is the serpent of chaos who attacks the solar barque, teaching that darkness returns every night and must be resisted again.
### 08.04 Apshai 🪲 — Apshai is the insect or scarab-like divine power of lesser sacred life, teaching that even small creatures can carry cosmic symbolism.
### 08.05 Bast 🐈 — Bast is the cat goddess of protection, pleasure, and household grace, teaching that tenderness and claws belong to the same guardian.
### 08.06 Bes 😄 — Bes is the dwarf protector of homes, childbirth, and laughter, teaching that joy can be a shield against fear.
### 08.07 Geb 🌍 — Geb is the earth father whose body is the land, teaching that every kingdom stands on a living god.
### 08.08 Horus 🦅 — Horus is the falcon son who avenges Osiris and claims kingship, teaching that rightful rule is born from grief, struggle, and restoration.
### 08.09 Isis 🪄 — Isis is the magician-mother who restores Osiris and protects Horus, teaching that love armed with wisdom can reassemble a broken world.
### 08.10 Nephthys 🕯️ — Nephthys is the mourning sister and funerary guardian, teaching that compassion may come from the shadowed side of the family.
### 08.11 Osiris 🌾 — Osiris is the murdered and resurrected lord of the dead, teaching that betrayal can become the seed of eternal renewal.
### 08.12 Ptah 🛠️ — Ptah is the craftsman creator who shapes reality through heart and word, teaching that imagination spoken with skill becomes world.
### 08.13 Seker ⚰️ — Seker is the silent funerary and necropolis power, teaching that the deepest passage of death is guarded by stillness.
### 08.14 Set 🌪️ — Set is the desert storm and murderer of Osiris, teaching that chaos can be kin, rival, protector, and destroyer at once.
### 08.15 Shu 💨 — Shu is the air god who separates sky from earth, teaching that life begins when space is made between embracing powers.
### 08.16 Tefnut 💦 — Tefnut is moisture, rain, and balance, teaching that the world's breath depends on the return of living water.
### 08.17 Thoth 🖋️ — Thoth is the ibis-headed scribe of wisdom, moon, and measure, teaching that writing preserves the order even gods must obey.

## 10. ⚡ Greek Mythos
- Story Lens: Passion, pride, beauty, punishment, monsters, heroic arrogance.
- Tone: Bright, dramatic, human, theatrical.

### 10.01 Zeus ⚡ — Zeus is the thunder king who overthrows the Titans and rules Olympus, teaching that order may be born from rebellion yet remain flawed by desire.
### 10.02 Aphrodite 🌹 — Aphrodite is the goddess of love and beauty, teaching that desire can bless, expose, and overthrow even the strongest wills.
### 10.03 Apollo 🎵 — Apollo is the god of sun, music, prophecy, and plague, teaching that beauty and clarity can still wound when pride enters the lyre.
### 10.04 Ares 🩸 — Ares is the god of brutal war, teaching that battle-lust without wisdom makes even divine strength look small.
### 10.05 Artemis 🏹 — Artemis is the huntress and moon goddess who guards wildness and chastity, teaching that boundaries deserve reverence.
### 10.06 Athena 🦉 — Athena is the goddess of wisdom, craft, and strategic war, teaching that intelligence is the sharpest spear.
### 10.07 Atlas 🌍 — Atlas is the Titan condemned to bear the heavens, teaching that rebellion can leave a body holding the weight of the world.
### 10.08 Circe 🧪 — Circe is the enchantress who transforms men into beasts, teaching that magic reveals the animal already hidden inside pride.
### 10.09 Cyclops 👁️ — The Cyclopes are one-eyed giant smiths and monsters, teaching that primal craft and savage appetite can share the same enormous face.
### 10.10 Demeter 🌾 — Demeter is the grain mother who mourns Persephone, teaching that grief can change the seasons of the entire world.
### 10.11 Dionysus 🍇 — Dionysus is the god of wine, ecstasy, theater, and madness, teaching that repression breaks before the god who frees and frenzies the soul.
### 10.12 Epimetheus 📦 — Epimetheus is the after-thinker who receives Pandora, teaching that thoughtless gifts can open every sorrow into the world.
### 10.13 Furies 🐍 — The Furies are avenging spirits of blood-guilt, teaching that crimes against kin call justice even from beneath the earth.
### 10.14 Hades 🏛️ — Hades is the lord of the underworld, teaching that death is not evil but a kingdom with laws, wealth, and inevitability.
### 10.15 Hecate 🔥 — Hecate is the goddess of crossroads, ghosts, and witchcraft, teaching that power waits where paths split and torches burn at night.
### 10.16 Hecatoncheires 💯 — The Hecatoncheires are hundred-handed giants who help overthrow the Titans, teaching that imprisoned strength can decide the war of gods.
### 10.17 Hephaestus ⚒️ — Hephaestus is the lame smith of Olympus, teaching that rejection can forge beauty, weapons, and cunning no perfect body could make.
### 10.18 Hera 👑 — Hera is the queen of Olympus and guardian of marriage, teaching that wounded dignity can become both law and vengeance.
### 10.19 Heracles 🦁 — Heracles is the laboring hero who suffers madness, guilt, and impossible tasks, teaching that redemption is earned one monster at a time.
### 10.20 Hermes 🪽 — Hermes is the messenger, thief, and guide of souls, teaching that wit travels faster than power and crosses every border.
### 10.21 Kronos ⏳ — Kronos is the Titan who devours his children to prevent prophecy, teaching that fear of succession creates the very rebellion it dreads.
### 10.22 Nike 🏆 — Nike is victory personified, teaching that triumph is a winged force that blesses the bold but never stands still.
### 10.23 Oceanus 🌊 — Oceanus is the world-encircling Titan river, teaching that all lands are bordered by mystery.
### 10.24 Odysseus 🧭 — Odysseus is the cunning king who wanders home after Troy, teaching that survival depends on wit, endurance, and the longing for home.
### 10.25 Pan 🐐 — Pan is the goat-foot god of wild music, panic, and shepherd places, teaching that nature laughs before civilization can explain it.
### 10.26 Perseus 🪞 — Perseus is the hero who slays Medusa and rescues Andromeda, teaching that courage needs gifts, timing, and a clear mirrored gaze.
### 10.27 Poseidon 🔱 — Poseidon is the sea and earthquake god, teaching that the world beneath ships and cities is never fully tame.
### 10.28 Prometheus 🔥 — Prometheus is the Titan who steals fire for humanity and suffers for it, teaching that compassion may defy heaven and pay eternally.
### 10.29 Theseus 🧵 — Theseus is the maze-walking hero who slays the Minotaur, teaching that civic glory begins by facing the monster hidden in the house.
### 10.30 Tyche 🎲 — Tyche is fortune personified, teaching that cities and lives turn on a wheel no mortal fully controls.
