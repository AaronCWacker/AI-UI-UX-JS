DIY Electroforming Setup & Recipe

SAFETY FIRST: Always add ACID to WATER, never water to acid. Wear gloves, eye protection, and work in a ventilated area (like your workshop/garage).

1. The Electrolyte Recipe (1 Liter)

  • Distilled Water: 750ml (Base)
  • Copper Sulfate Pentahydrate: 200g (The "Metal" source)
  • 98% Sulfuric Acid: 40ml - 50ml (Increases conductivity)
  • Copper Brightener: 5-10 drops (Ensures a shiny finish vs. dull salmon color)

2. Step-by-Step Process

  1. Dissolve: Mix the Copper Sulfate into the room-temp distilled water until fully dissolved.
  2. Acidify: Slowly pour the sulfuric acid into the blue solution. It will get warm.
  3. Brighten: Once cool, add your brightener drops.
  4. Prep the Piece: Seal your Pyrite, paint with Graphite Conductive Paint, and attach a copper "lead" wire (Cathode).
  5. Plate: Submerge the piece. Hang a Copper Anode on the opposite side. Set your power supply to low amperage (approx 0.1A per square inch of surface area).
