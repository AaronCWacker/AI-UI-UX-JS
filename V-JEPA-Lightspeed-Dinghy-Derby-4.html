🛸 Einstein's Light Postulate

Push toward light-speed and your clock slows, the track contracts ahead of you, and colors Doppler-shift. Yet everyone — you and every rival — always measures light at the same speed c. That single stubborn fact is what bends space and time.

Your agent watches the track and trains a world model. At first it's reactive. Once it learns, it can predict the consequences of its actions and plan corners ahead — LeCun's case that real agents must forecast, not just react.
